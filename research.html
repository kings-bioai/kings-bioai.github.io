Research

Computation as the instrument.

We combine statistical genetics, machine learning and multi-omics to investigate the molecular mechanisms underlying ALS and motor neuron degeneration — from population-scale genomes to single cells.

01 — Statistical genetics & GWAS

The genetics of risk and resilience.

We run genome-wide association and rare-variant analyses across familial and sporadic ALS to locate the variants that raise — and lower — disease risk, and to build polygenic scores that quantify it.

GWAS meta-analysisRare variantsPolygenic scoresGene × environment
02 — Machine learning & AI

Models that anticipate the disease.

Statistical and deep-learning models stratify patients, predict progression and discover biomarkers from multi-modal data — with interpretability treated as a first-class requirement, not an afterthought.

Deep learningPredictive modellingSubtypingFeature attribution
03 — Multi-omics integration

Many layers, one biology.

We develop databases and pipelines that harmonise genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic and clinical data, so that signals invisible in any single layer emerge once the layers are aligned.

Data harmonisationPipelinesDatabase developmentIntegration
↳ Also in the lab

Adjacent methods.

Approaches we bring to bear when the question calls for them.

04

Transcriptomics

Bulk and single-cell RNA-seq with spatial transcriptomics to characterise expression patterns, cellular heterogeneity and disease-specific signatures in motor neurons — including cell-type deconvolution and differential expression.

05

Network biology

Systems-level analysis of protein–protein and co-expression networks, pathway enrichment, and the identification of disease-relevant network modules that connect individual variants to shared mechanisms.

06

Structural modelling

Structure prediction and molecular-dynamics simulation of ALS-associated proteins — TDP-43, SOD1, FUS and C9orf72 — to study aggregation and prioritise drug-binding hypotheses.

↳ Current projects

In progress.

Active initiatives advancing how ALS is understood, stratified and treated.

→

Multi-omics stratification of ALS

Integrating genomic, transcriptomic and proteomic data across more than a thousand patients to define molecular subtypes, discover biomarkers and predict progression toward personalised strategies.

→

A single-cell atlas of motor-neuron degeneration

Building a single-cell transcriptomic atlas from ALS patients and controls to map cellular heterogeneity, locate vulnerable populations and characterise disease-specific expression programmes at high resolution.

→

Genetic modifiers in C9orf72 ALS

Investigating modifiers that shape onset and progression in C9orf72 repeat-expansion carriers — searching for protective variants and the mechanisms behind phenotypic variability.

→

AI-driven drug repurposing

Combining network-based drug–target prediction with machine learning over large-scale multi-omics data to prioritise existing compounds for repurposing in ALS.

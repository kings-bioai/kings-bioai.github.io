Three questions,
one approach.

Computation is our microscope. We treat the genome, the transcriptome, and the clinic as one connected dataset — and read across them.

01 — Statistical genetics

The genetics of risk and resilience.

Genome-wide association, rare-variant analysis and polygenic scoring across familial and sporadic ALS — including the oligogenic architecture now reshaping how risk is understood and tested.

02 — Machine learning

Models that anticipate the disease.

Statistical and deep-learning models for stratifying patients, predicting progression, and surfacing biomarkers from multi-modal clinical and genetic data — built to be interpretable, not just accurate.

Progression predictionStratificationBiomarkersInterpretability
Explore →
03 — Multi-omics integration

Many layers, one biology.

Pipelines that harmonise genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic and clinical data so that signals invisible in any single layer become legible when the layers are read together.
